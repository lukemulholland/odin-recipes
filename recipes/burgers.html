Description

Burgers are the ideal weekend lunch. Follow my recipe for a quick and easy lunchtime treat that everyone will love.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound ground beef (80/20 fat ratio is recommended for juiciness)
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 hamburger bun per burger
  • Optional: Lettuce, tomato, onion, cheese, ketchup, mustard, mayonnaise

Steps

  1. In a large bowl, gently combine ground beef, salt, and pepper. Avoid overworking the meat, as this can make the burgers tough.
  2. Divide the meat mixture into equal portions and form them into patties slightly wider than your hamburger buns. Make a dimple in the center of each patty with your thumb to prevent them from puffing up while cooking.
  3. Heat a grill or pan over medium-high heat. If using a grill, lightly oil the grates. Cook the patties for 3-4 minutes per side, or until desired doneness. An instant-read thermometer inserted into the center of the patty should read 160°F (71°C) for medium-rare.
  4. While the burgers are cooking, toast the hamburger buns if desired. You can toast them on the grill or in a toaster.
  5. Assemble your burgers! Place the cooked patty on the bottom bun, then add your favorite toppings. Top with the other half of the bun and enjoy!
